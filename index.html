About me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Michigan State University with a strong background in machine learning and natural language processing. My research focuses on developing methods for extracting patterns from small, complex datasets, with applications in healthcare, language, and data analysis.

I have published at NAACL and demonstrated the ability to lead interdisciplinary research projects and collaborate across domains. My work bridges the gap between theoretical advances in AI and practical applications that can benefit underserved communities.

Research Interests

  • AI in Healthcare

    Leveraging large language models to generalize patterns learned from scarce medical datasets, enabling better predictions and insights for underserved populations.

  • Long-Tail Generation

    Investigating and enhancing how large language models understand and generate rare linguistic phenomena, including idioms and figurative language.

  • AI in Education

    Developing intelligent systems that personalize classroom experiences by tailoring lessons to individual student needs and learning styles.
